Abstract

Multi-objective reinforcement learning (MORL) is a powerful tool to learn Pareto-optimal policy families across conflicting objectives. However, unlike traditional RL algorithms, existing MORL algorithms do not leverage large-scale parallelization to concurrently simulate thousands of environments, resulting in vastly increased computation time and hindering performance in scenarios with many objectives. Ultimately, this has limited MORL's application towards robotics problems, which often feature complex multi-objective learning landscapes. To address these challenges, we present MO-Playground: a pip-installable playground of GPU-accelerated multi-objective robotic environments compatible with MORLAX, a new GPU-native, fast MORL algorithm. Together, MO-Playground and MORLAX approximate Pareto Sets within minutes, offering an average 25-270 speed-up compared to legacy CPU-based approaches whilst achieving superior Pareto Front hypervolumes. In addition to gaining insights into key design choices in GPU-based MORL, we demonstrate the versatility of our approach by implementing a custom BRUCE humanoid robot environment using MO-Playground and learning Pareto-optimal locomotion policies across 6 realistic objectives for BRUCE, such as smoothness, efficiency and arm swinging.
